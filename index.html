Abstract

Humans excel at forecasting the future dynamics of a scene given just a single image. Video prediction models that can mimic this ability are an essential component for intelligent systems. Recent approaches have improved temporal coherence and 3D consistency in single-image-conditioned video prediction. However, these methods often lack robust user controllability, such as modifying the camera path, limiting their applicability in real-world applications. Existing state-of-the-art camera-controlled image-to-video models face challenges in accurately modeling camera motion, ensuring temporal consistency, and preserving geometric integrity. Leveraging explicit intermediate 3D or 4D representations promises to eliminate these limitations, by ensuring the predicted video depicts a coherent scene from a specific camera trajectory. We propose a novel framework that predicts a 4D Gaussian Splat representation from a single image in a single forward pass within seconds, enabling the rendering of videos with camera guidance. Extensive experiments on the KITTI and Waymo datasets demonstrate that our method achieves state-of-the-art video quality and inference efficiency performance.
